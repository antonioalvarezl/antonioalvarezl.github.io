I am a PhD student at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (affiliated with Instituto de Ciencias Matemáticas) and Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg. My research focuses on the mathematical foundations of machine learning, specifically on the analysis and control of continuous-time neural network architectures such as neural ODEs and flow-based generative models.

Currently, I am visiting my PhD advisor Prof. Enrique Zuazua at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg, following a very fruitful research stay at the National University of Singapore (August – November 2025), where I worked with Prof. Qianxiao Li on advanced topics in machine learning theory.

Updates:

  • January 2025: New preprint on arXiv! In collaboration with Borjan Geshkovski and Domènec Ruiz-Balet, we uploaded our work "Perceptrons and localization of attention's mean-field landscape". We study the forward pass of Transformers as interacting particle systems on the sphere, analyzing how the perceptron block affects the mean-field limit and showing that its critical points are generically atomic and localized.
  • December 2025: I attended the IMS International Conference on Statistics and Data Science (ICSDS) in Seville. I had the honor of receiving the Student Travel Award from Richard Samworth in person. I also presented my talk "Continuous-Time Learning of Probability Distributions via Neural ODEs with Applications in Continuous Glucose Monitoring Data". It was a fantastic experience connecting with the statistics community and enjoying the plenary sessions in such a lovely city!

  • November 2025: I completed my research stay at the National University of Singapore. It was an incredibly productive period where I learned a lot and met brilliant researchers. I am now back at FAU Erlangen-Nürnberg visiting my PhD advisor, while working on a couple of ongoing projects from NUS that I'm very excited about.

  • October 2025: New preprint on arXiv! In collaboration with fellow PhD student Martin Hernández, we uploaded our work "Convergence, design and training of continuous-time dropout as a random batch method". We study the dropout technique for continuous-time models as random batch methods for interacting particles, providing a novel theoretical framework.

  • October 2025: I decided to create this personal website to share my research and academic activities.

  • September 2025: I received the Student Travel Award at the IMS International Conference on Statistics and Data Science (ICSDS) 2025. I will be presenting my work at the conference in mid December.

  • September 2025: I gave two seminars on "Entropy-driven control of the continuity equation for normalizing flows":

  • August 2025: I started a 3-month research visit at the National University of Singapore, working with Prof. Qianxiao Li's group on mathematical foundations of machine learning.

I am a PhD student at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (affiliated with Instituto de Ciencias Matemáticas) and Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg. My research focuses on the mathematical foundations of machine learning, specifically on the analysis and control of continuous-time neural network architectures such as transformers and flow-based generative models.

Currently, I am a visiting researcher at the National University of Singapore, working with Prof. Qianxiao Li on advanced topics in machine learning theory and scientific computing.

Updates:

  • October 2025: New preprint on arXiv! In collaboration with fellow PhD student Martin Hernández, we uploaded our preprint "Convergence, design and training of continuous-time dropout as a random batch method". In this work, we study the dropout technique for continuous-time models as random batch methods for interacting particles, providing a novel theoretical framework.
